Introduction

Breeding 4.0 seeks to enhance breeding efficiency and precision by combining digital and biological technologies to achieve a faster, more efficient and more accurate breeding process. relies on big data, artificial intelligence, machine learning and genome editing technologies to collect, integrate and analyse large amounts of crop genomic, phenotypic and environmental data to predict phenotypic traits in plants and to target breeding on this basis.

We have developed BreedingAI, a user-friendly database that provides breeders and machine learning experts with easy access to genome-phenotype pair data. It provide two types of unstructured data: GVCF(genomic VCF) files and VCF files, along with one type of structured data which is extracted from vcf files by GSCtool, to facilitate research in breeding machine learning.

Statistics

You can find out more details about the data we have collected in this module.

Rice

Rice, a paramount staple crop, is represented by a collection of 129,449 phenotypic records. This comprehensive assembly spans 12 distinct regions, spanning the years from 2011 through 2018 and extending to 2020. Encompassing a spectrum of 41 distinct traits, these phenotypic data illuminate facets ranging from growth patterns to yield attributes, thereby affording a holistic grasp of rice's developmental and productive expressions.

Soybean

Soybean, renowned for its significance as both a proteinaceous and oleaginous crop, contributes 284,395 phenotypic entries. This dataset spans cultivation regions across five distinct locales, covering a span of years encompassing 2013 through 2019. Across these years, 114 diverse phenotypic features are documented, forming a mosaic that captures the crop's multifaceted attributes.

Zea

Maize, holding eminence as a vital food and fodder source, lends its essence with 16,284 phenotypic profiles. These profiles encapsulate three geographical domains and span the years 2016 and 2017, illuminating 18 distinct phenotypic attributes.

Download

Our data collection efforts have focused on three major crop species, and we have transformed the collected data into three distinct formats: two unstructured data types, namely GVCF and VCF, and one structured data type called GSCtool features. These datasets, in conjunction with the available phenotypic data, hold significant potential to drive advancements in the realm of breeding machine learning.

By incorporating these comprehensive datasets, researchers can leverage them to develop innovative tools and models in the field. The integration of genomic information (GVCF and VCF) and structured features (GSCtool) with phenotypic data provides a robust foundation for advancing breeding machine learning techniques and contributing to the progress of the field as a whole.

ML

Feature Extraction

The engine of the feature extraction module is GSCtool, which is designed as a flexible tool for extracting features from different types of SNPs variation detection result files. Currently, three major crops are supported: rice, soybean and maize, based on IRGSP 1.0, Williams 82 and B73 reference genomes, respectively. gsctool is easy to operate, and users can upload the corresponding variant detection result files for feature extraction. For other reference genomes, users can use gsctool with the corresponding gff3 files for customised feature extraction. This module enables users to extract key information from genomic variation data, laying a solid foundation for subsequent machine learning model construction.

Model Construction

The machine learning model building module combines the power of optuna and lightgbm to provide users with a highly customisable model optimisation framework. Within a predefined hyperparameter space, users can adjust the hyperparameters according to their specific needs and upload data files obtained from previous feature extraction for model construction. optuna will search the hyperparameter space to find the best model configuration. lightgbm, a fast and efficient gradient boosting framework, is able to efficiently process large-scale data and generate accurate predictive models. The labels in the data files uploaded by the user will be considered as prediction targets, thus enabling the model construction and prediction processes to be closely linked.
